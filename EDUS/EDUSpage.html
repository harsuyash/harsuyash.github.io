INTRODUCTION

Project Background

This is done from home in the times of COVID 19 and a relavant problem to deal with, for me, was about online education. The project was done under the guidance of Prof. Swati Pal and mainly focused on engagement in online education through interactive tools. The final product mostly focuses on the discussion forums and interactive (teacher-student and peer to peer) part of online education and attempts to encourage participation by everyone and keep the discussions focused on learning.

Initial Topic: Online teaching of school children and its effectiveness

The Concern- Online edication beyond pandemic

In the face of a pandemic we have been forced to accept online education and many challenges have come to surface. But beyond pandemic, having developed an infrastructure and adapted to learning models for online education will only be beneficial. Learning can cross the boundaries of time and space and we can move beyond textbook learning towards more interactive, practical learning.

Shift in pedagogy: Sage on stage vs guide by the side

Traditional way of teaching involves lecturing that aims at transfers of knowledge. A teacher has more control over the class and monitors what the students are doing . The landscape and the dynamics are different online and there is a need for more shared and collaborative learning.

Summary of the problem analysis

Current scenario

Teachers have less control over students. Students find it hard to be attentive and there is a missing connection in distant learning through screen. There is a need for shared responsibilities and collaboration.

Existing solutions

Some solutions offer a platform to create interactive content and a space for discussions/ brainstorming and some solutions even provide tools for formative assessment of students.

The gap

Students are still not motivated and assessments are somewhat forced on them. There is polarization in the interactions. Some teachers find it overwhelming and a waste of time, and want to complete the syllabus.

Our goals

Reducing the polarity by encouraging participation and regulating the discussion to keep it focused towards learning objectives. Teachers should be able to easily create content and moderate discussions.

Focusing on interactivity- where is the gap?

Almost everyone agreeson the importance of active learning in onlin eenvironment and there are many web/ app solutions to make the lecture more interactive and engaging, yet the teachers and students are not willing to use them. I dived deeper to understand the problems that people face in such online interactive environment, discussion forums, formative asseessment applications, etc. After refleting back on the users's responses, I induced the following insights:

There is polarization in the discussions where some students take part activiely, some just prank or spam and some are passive and zone out.

It is difficult for the teachers to keep the discussions focused and track learning and they consider interactive sessions a waste of time.

A lot of existing solutions are overwhelming to use for the teachers

Students are not motivated and the interactive assessments are forced.

Subjects like languages and History are slightly more difficult to teach.

From Review of Literature

I went through some existing research and proven concepts for engagement and came acsross literature on cognitive engagement which aligns wih our goal and decided to keep it as a framework for generating ideas and evaluating them.

Brainstorming and free flowing ideas

Breaking down goals into concrete elements

The current goals is still quite abstract and broad. In order to come up with consrete ideas, I decided to break it down further into more concrete elements by asking "WHY?"s and taking help from secondary research. For each of the concrete answer to the "WHY?"s, I ideated the "HOW?"s and clustered them. You can see the process below.

Motivation to pay attention and participate

Factor 1- Fear of Judgement/ being right or wrong

Judgement of individuals not ideas

Stage fright/ too much attention

Too much emphasis on performance

Possible solutions for factor 1

Creating a perception cloud to show variations

Shared participation/ supporting students if they are stuck

Take away point in the end to show there is something to learn

Factor 2- At loss of what to say/ ask

Too open ended/ it’s outside the text

Not listening carefully or not questioning what they are told

Possible solutions for factor 2

Giving existing prompts to facilitate thinking

Distributing Question Cookies at the start to find answers
Stream of thought- adding on to the previous person

Factor 3- Simply not interested and feel forced

Too rigid and academic structure

Possible solutions for factor 2

Borrowing from social media behaviour, doing what they like
Categorical assessment instead of ordinal

Strategy to learn and think critically

Trigger- exploration- integration and resolution (Garrison, 2001)

Make predictions, existing perception, reasoning (Zhu, 2006)

Debating, negotiating and meaning making (Zhu, 2006)

Rehearsal, summarize, elaborate, organize (Corno & Mandinach, 1983)

Easy creation and moderation by teacher

Too many options are overwhelming are take up time to explore

Having toolbars and too many menu items make it difficult to learn

Having a modular template that can be easily edited without much tool

Chunking the options in groups of 3-4 to reduce cognitive load
